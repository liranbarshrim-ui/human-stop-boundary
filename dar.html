DAR — Decision Authority Record

When a decision becomes irreversible, who is named to stop it?

Not a team. Not a process. A person who will be held accountable.

If no one is named, no one is responsible.

Exposure If an irreversible decision causes harm, responsibility will not remain abstract.

It will resolve to a name.

If no name was defined in advance, accountability will be assigned after the fact.
The gap Every organization has people who approve. No organization names the person required to stop.

When no name exists, no stop exists. The system continues until it cannot be undone.
What DAR does

One named person. One pathway. One commitment.

DAR identifies the single individual who must be named — by name, not by role — as the stop authority for a specific irreversible decision pathway.

Not accountability after the fact. Named commitment before execution becomes impossible to reverse.

Engagement deliverable
Named Stop Authority Map for one decision pathway
Mapped to an existing decision pathway inside the organization (not theoretical).
One name. In writing. Before it moves.
Gap analysis: where the stop function is missing or shared
Delivered in 7 to 10 business days
Who this is for

Personal exposure. Board accountability. Irreversible decisions.

CMOs. Medical Directors. Chief Risk Officers. Heads of compliance with personal regulatory liability.

If your name is on the line when something cannot be undone, DAR is for you.

Independent engagement. Direct accountability. No delegation layer.
