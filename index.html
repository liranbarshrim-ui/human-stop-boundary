Pre-Incident Responsibility Definition for Autonomous Systems
PRINCIPLE

The Authority to Initiate is not the Authority to Terminate.

DEFINITION

A Decision Accountability Record (DAR) is an immutable, auditable log of Who, What, Why, and When for any critical system action.

WHY DAR EXISTS

The 2012 Knight Capital event was not a technical failure.

It was a failure of stopping authority.

The system continued to execute because no single person had the structural mandate to terminate the process.

REGULATORY REALITY

When a system fails, regulators do not investigate the algorithm. They ask who was responsible.

Who holds the authority to stop the system when execution becomes irreversible?
BY NAME

The DAR framework establishes a named human authority before irreversible automated execution.
