Decision Accountability Record (DAR)

Matrix Audit is an independent governance initiative focused on structural accountability in autonomous systems.

Decision Accountability Record (DAR) defines named human stop authority in automated systems operating in high-risk environments.

PRINCIPLE

The Authority to Initiate is not the Authority to Terminate.

Automated systems may execute decisions. Accountability remains human.

FORMAL DEFINITION

Decision Accountability Record (DAR) is a governance principle assigning named human authority responsible for stopping automated systems before irreversible execution occurs.

PURPOSE

DAR ensures that automated systems operating in critical environments cannot execute irreversible actions without an identified human authority responsible for halting execution.

REFERENCE CASE

The 2012 Knight Capital event demonstrated how automated systems can execute catastrophic actions when no clearly defined human authority exists to stop execution.

KEY QUESTION

Who holds the authority to stop the system when execution becomes irreversible?

By name.

When a decision becomes irreversible under uncertainty, someone must have the authority to stop it — by name.
