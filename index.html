Principle

Autonomous systems execute decisions.

Responsibility remains human.

At some point every automated system reaches a boundary where execution becomes irreversible.

The Decision Accountability Record identifies the individual who holds the authority to stop the system before that boundary is reached.

Definition
Decision Accountability Record (DAR)

A governance artifact created prior to system execution. It records the individual who holds explicit authority to halt a system when it reaches an irreversible decision boundary.

The DAR exists prior to the event. It defines responsibility in advance.

Not after failure.

Why DAR exists

Most governance frameworks define policies, controls, and review processes.

Few define the specific moment when authority must attach to a person.

DAR addresses that gap directly. It identifies the point where a system's decision becomes irreversible and records who holds the authority to stop it.

Regulatory Reality

When a system fails, regulators do not investigate the algorithm.
They ask who was responsible.

Who holds the authority to stop the system
when execution becomes irreversible?
