A governance structure defining named human stop authority in autonomous systems.

Principle

The Authority to Initiate is not the Authority to Terminate.

Systems may execute decisions. Accountability remains human.

Formal Definition

Decision Accountability Record (DAR): A governance structure assigning named human authority responsible for stopping automated systems before irreversible execution.

Purpose

DAR ensures that automated systems operating in critical environments cannot execute irreversible actions without an identified human authority responsible for halting execution.

Governance Domains

DAR applies to governance structures in financial systems, AI operations, autonomous infrastructure, algorithmic trading systems, and other environments where automated actions may produce irreversible outcomes.

Reference Case

The 2012 Knight Capital event demonstrated how automated systems can execute catastrophic actions when no clearly defined human authority exists to stop execution.

Key Concepts

Human Stop Authority – the explicit mandate granted to a named individual to halt automated execution when necessary.

Irreversible Execution – automated actions that cannot be safely reversed once initiated.

Governance Boundary – the structural point where automated execution must yield to human authority.

DAR Structure

1. System under governance
2. Irreversible execution boundary
3. Named human stop authority
4. Conditions under which stop authority may be exercised

Research Context

The DAR concept is part of the Human Stop Boundary research initiative exploring governance boundaries for irreversible automated decisions.

FAQ

What is DAR?
DAR is a governance structure that assigns a named human authority responsible for stopping automated systems before irreversible execution occurs.

Why is DAR necessary?
Without clearly defined stop authority, automated systems may execute irreversible decisions without human accountability.

Where can DAR be applied?
DAR can be applied in AI governance, financial trading systems, industrial automation, and autonomous infrastructure.
