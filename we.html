The Death of "We"

I

The Death of "We"

In the boardroom, everyone says "We."

"We approved."

"We're moving forward."

It sounds safe. It feels aligned.

It isn't.

When something goes wrong, "We" disappears. No one is responsible.

II

The Illusion of Safety

Consensus is mistaken for control.

If enough people agree, it must be correct.

If enough people approve, it must be safe.

No one feels the weight of stopping.

And when no one feels it, no one stops it.
III

The Missing Function

Every system moves forward.

Unless someone is required to stop.

Without someone explicitly required to stop, there is only motion.
IV

What Actually Fails

Failures rarely begin with intent.

They begin with motion.

A process starts.

Momentum builds.

No one interrupts.

And so it continues.

V

The Line No One Sees

Systems do not fail at decision.

They fail when decisions become irreversible.

This is not a cliff. It is a slope.
VI

The Structural Shift

Replace "We" with a name.

One person required to stop.

That is exactly why it is avoided.

When a decision becomes irreversible under uncertainty, is there a name?

Not a role. Not a committee.

A name.

If not, the system will continue.

Until it doesn't.
