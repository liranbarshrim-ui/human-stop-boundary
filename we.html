The Death of "We"

I

In the boardroom, everyone says "We."

"We approved."

"We're moving forward."

It sounds safe.

It isn't.

Responsibility shared by everyone is responsibility avoided by anyone It behaves like gas It expands It applies no pressure

II

Consensus is mistaken for control.

And when no one feels it, no one stops it.

III

Initiate Review Approve Execute Stop — missing
Without someone required to stop, there is only motion.

IV

A process starts.

Momentum builds.

No one interrupts.

V

"We can still stop"
↓
No signal
↓
"We are already committed"
This is not a cliff. It is a slope.

VI

Replace "We" with a name.

One person required to stop.

That is exactly why it is avoided.
When a decision becomes irreversible under uncertainty, is there a name?
